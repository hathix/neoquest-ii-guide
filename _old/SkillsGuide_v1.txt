<a name="introduction"><h3 align="center">Introduction</h3></a>
<p>Welcome to my Skills Guide! If you're looking for the complete walkthrough of NQII, go to <a 

href="neopets.com/~ThePhoenixRising">ThePhoenixRising's page</a>. This guide will instruct you on 

how to spend the skill points you get while playing NQII (ohemgee really? I never would have 

guessed.)</p>
<p>Basically, each time a character levels up, he/she gets a skill point to spend in any of seven 

categories. These categories can increase the character's power, speed, or fortitude, or they can 

let your character use a new special ability. In this guide, I'll be telling you what skills I 

recommend and how to spend them.</p>
<p>If you looked at the Jump, a lot of things probably didn't make sense. First off, I'll have my 

comments on each characters' skills. In "Recommended Skill Levels", I'll tell you what I recommend 

as the final skill point spread for each character. In "Level Chart", you'll see which skills to 

invest in after each level. In "Skill Level Checkpoints", you'll be reminded what your skill 

spreads should look like after every few levels. Let's begin, shall we?</p>

<a name="rohane"><h3 align="center">Skills for Rohane</h3></a>


<a name="levelchart"><h3 align="center">Level Chart</h3></a>
<p>This is the most useful part of the guide, in my opinion. Everything else is just commentary. 

When you level up, you may be asking yourself where to put the skill point you just got. Never 

fear, Rev is here! With this chart, you'll know where to spend your points as soon as you level up. 

*hits button* That was easy.</p>
<p>Oh yeah, and BTW, the "Level" on the table indicates the level you grow to. For example, if you 

just got to level 10, check out Level 10 on the chart. If I write "DI 1", I mean to spend a point 

in Damage Increase, thereby getting that skill to level 1. And the skills I advocate below work 

well on all difficulties (I've tried it.)</p>
