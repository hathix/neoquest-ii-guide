northern watchtower

For once, a tower that *isn't* owned by a megalomaniacal jerk.

When you're plopped into Faerieland, switch to Normal mode and follow the path south until you see a conviently-placed tower blocking your way. Go ahead and enter it. Once you're there, take a gander at your map for the place. A Scorchio called Calam on the first floor sells weapons. To get to Gustano, the potion selling Elephante, go to the 2nd floor. Oh yeah, look at your shopping list too.

Shopping List

  • Milk
  • Eggs
  • Cheese
  • Potions:
    • 20 Typhoon Potions
    • 20 Slumber Potions
    • 20 Speed of Wind Potions
    • 1 to 3 Vivify Potions (if you're out of Resurrection Potions from Act 1)
  • Weapons/Armor:
    • As usual, get what you need (i.e. only buy if the weapon is a big step up from what you normally have.) The upgrades here are good, but not amazing or anything.
  • Inn: No point resting here, since you just started the Act.

Not-so-fun-fact: This is the 2nd-last town to buy weapons/armor and the 3rd-last place to buy potions. There are only 2 bosses between this place and the next town, though, so it's all cool. Anyway, look at your map for Faerieland: Map 2 or whatever it's called. This is the path to the next boss. Any way you go will get you to the boss, just choose a path before you set out.

We want to grow to level 51/52/53 (remember: don't spend the skill point) before we get to the boss, so use that old Fight-As-You-Go method. Once you level up, keep fighting baddies, since more fighting now equals less fighting later. Well, head out, going south on the cloud path thing until you reach your next boss, the Fallen Angel. *dun dun dun*

fallen angel

I think she's got the Force in her hand. That's pretty sweet.
Recommended Level: 51 on Normal
52 on Evil
53 on Insane
HP: 1200 on Normal
1500 on Evil
1800 on Insane
Special Abilities:
Renew 150HP Heal
Spellbind Mesmerization
Shockwave
Stunning Strikes
Prizes: 2 weapons/armor
3000 gold
1000 NP
  • Strategy:
  • General: She's not too tough, really. If you feel like walking back to the Watchtower, you can use the potions you just got, else just use Haunted Woods Potions.
  • Setup: Toss some Vile's Apathy Potions or whatever potions you have at the start to slow her. Make sure Mipsy has Group Haste up at all times and Velm has Group Shields up. If you ever forget to do that, these bosses will eat you and I'll eat you too. Ha ha.
  • Offensive: Don't bother with damage potions: even though she can heal, she doesn't have too much HP. Rohane and Talinia should use their melee (red Attack! button) and Mipsy should use Direct Damage. Pretty simple.
  • Defensive: Again, make sure Velm has those Group Shields up. He should use Group Healing whenever someone is hurt. Whenever no one is hurt he can toss Blister Potions or something.

Post-battle:

If you used any potions from the Watchtower (i.e. Typhoon, Speed of Wind, or Slumber Potions), walk back there and restock. You need 20 of each of the Typhoon, Speed of Wind, and Slumber Potions before you go the next area.

the underclouds

Oh great, another depressing area.

If you look at your map for this place, it looks absolutely HUGE. Sure enough, it is. Anyhow, we need to get to Cumulonimbus in the southeast part of the map. We need to get there and grow to level 52/53/54 before we get there. The idea is to stay on the outside of the path as you walk. Go ahead and, uh, get to Cumulonimbus.

cumulonimbus

OK, I'm running out of cloud pictures.

Well, if you take a peek at the map for this city, you'll feel like punching me for getting you hooked onto NQII. *ducks, then gets into defensive crouch* No, really, it's not that bad. Look, I even stole borrowed a picture from meowpillay's NQII maps:

Yeah, you want to walk to the big red kitty on the east side of town. It's a long hike, so you should use the Fight-As-You-Go method to grow to level 53/54/55 by the time you get to the Devilpuss (aka the big red kitty.) Once you're ready, walk up to the guy and ask him why he didn't lend you Segways when you entered the city.

devilpuss

The stereotypical cat: angry, moody, and in need of a nail trimming.
Recommended Level: 53 on Normal
54 on Evil
55 on Insane
HP: 1500 on Normal
1875 on Evil
2250 on Insane
Special Abilities:
Critical Attacks
Shockwave
5% Slowing Strike
Prizes: Devilpuss Avatar
2 weapons/armor
2500 gold
1100 NP
  • Strategy:
  • General: Not much to say here. He's tough, but then again bosses are supposed to be tough. It can't heal, thankfully, although it can hit hard.
  • Setup: Have Rohane and Talinia toss some Vile's Apathy Potions until they work. Velm should put up Group Shields and Mipsy should caste Group Haste. Standard stuff.
  • Offensive: It doesn't have terribly high HP, plus it can't heal, which is always good. Rohane, Mipsy, and Talinia should use their usual attacks. No need to use damage potions unless you're losing and need to kill this guy off quickly.
  • Defensive: The Devilpuss can hit for over 100 damage if you're unlucky. That being said, Velm should be Group Healing all the time. Even if someone's just a little scratched Velm should heal. Not like he's got anything better to do anyway.

Post-battle:

After you beat him and go back to the map, you'll get this avatar:

Pretty nice. More importantly, you'll want to leave Cumulonimbus and head back out to the Underclouds. If you look at your map, you'll see we need to get to Cirrus. Again, stick to the outside of the path and follow your maps till you get to Cirrus. You might as well stay on Hunting mode until you get there.

cirrus

Hooray! For once, you aren't surrounded by people who want to kill you.

Yeah, this is the last town in the game, except for one potion-seller at the very end. So this is the last place to buy weapons. Speaking of buying stuff, look at your shopping list:

Shopping List

  • Milk
  • Eggs
  • Cheese
  • Potions:
    • 20 Hurricane Potions
    • 20 Coma Potions
    • 20 Speed of Light Potions
  • Weapons/Armor:
    • BUY EVERYTHING. Even if you don't think you're going to ever use some of the weapons/armor, buy them anyway, since they're the last ones you're guaranteed to get.
  • Inn: Unless you feel like walking back from the start of the act if you die, rest here.

Yeah, this is an important place to buy stuff. Make absolutely sure you rest at the inn, too. Other than that, there isn't much more to see in this town. Leave town and head north till you see stairs, at which point take them. You'll be on the Road to Faerie City, which is really easy to navigate. Keep walking and you'll get to Faerie City.
