Eh? Now what? Oh, it's you. Hi. If you're completely lost, click on the header to get back to where you once belonged (if you're lost, you'll want to check the main page.) If you haven't beaten Act 3 yet, go ahead and do that.

If you aren't lost and you have beaten Act 3, then you're probably in the right place. Congrats. Well, the Haunted Woods is a pretty small chapter (at least compared to the last one), and it shouldn't take too much time to get through. Have fun with this chapter, since the next chapter (which is the last) one is all business. Say, did I mention that the next chapter is the last one?

Before you start, keep in mind that this chapter is home to those diabolical Chias I've been telling you about. Be scared. Very scared.

shadow gulch

Who you gonna call? (Hint: not me.)

As soon as you land in the Haunted Woods, you'll notice a town just below you. Walk onto it, and you'll find out that it's infested with ghosts. Before you ask, no, you can't bust them. Sorry. The main tourist trap attraction here is the weapons/armor/potions store in the southwestern building. Walk on over there, then get out that shopping list.

Shopping List

  • Milk
  • Eggs
  • Cheese
  • Potions:
    • 20 Vile's Apathy Potions
    • 20 Rot Potions
    • 5 Kougra Sprint Potions
  • Weapons/Armor:
    • Chances are the bosses from the Lost Desert dropped good stuff, so we probably won't need to buy any weapons/armor from here. But if anyone's current weapon is 6 or so points weaker than the one for sale, you might want to upgrade.
  • Inn: Didn't we just rest at the start of the act? Yeah.

That's that for Shadow Gulch. Head out of town and get ready to use the Fight-As-You-Go method. Open up your map to the West Haunted Woods and look for Von Roo's Castle in the northeast corner. Head on over there, and enter as soon as you get there. (We don't need to level up just yet.)

von roo's castle

"What? No! Don't hit me with that stick! I'm not a pinata. These are just, uh, my street clothes. Wait, I don't wear clothes..."

Before you start doing anything, know that you should stay on Hunting for this entire castle. I don't care if you gain a level, two levels, or 42 levels... just stay on Hunting. It'll help. Believe me.

Now that that's over with, take a peek at the map of the Entrance and look for the stairs in the east side of the level. There's actually two ways to get to it, but it really doesn't matter one way or the other as long as you are on Hunting mode. (Sense a theme? Thought so.) By the time you get to the stairs, get to level 41/42/43 if you aren't at that level already. Climb the stairs once you're ready.

Now you're on the Dungeon level of the castle. It's a pretty simple route to Meuka, the boss of this castle. Yeah, you aren't fighting Von Roo. Go figure. But heal yourself and walk up to Meuka to pick your nose a fight.

Post-battle:

You can talk to Von Roo if you like, but take the teleporter outside. Stay on Hunting mode (actually, you'll want to stay on Hunting mode for the rest of this chapter), and head east into the East Haunted Woods. You might not notice it on your map, but there's a lot of impassable ground in this swamp. Just be aware of that.

You'll notice the entrance to a Cave of Dark Things at the end of this swamp; walk over to it. Make sure you're at least level 42/43/44 before you enter.

cave of dark things

Whaddya mean, "things?" You mean like this slightly disturbing rubber duck?

So, as I was saying, you want to stay on Hunting mode for all of this cave and the rest of the chapter. I'm feeling lazy now so I'll stop repeating it. Anyhow, we need to grow to level 44/45/46 - yeah, two levels - before we take on the boss at the end of this cave. Just keep that in mind.

Take a peek at your map and look for the conveniently placed slime puddles. Make your way through the cave, following those puddles. Once you get to the boss at the end of the cave, you'll hopefully have at least one more level under your belt. Train (yeah, that's what you did back before you fought Zombom... good times, good times) till you get to level 44/45/46.

Once you've gained that level, walk up to the spider boss guy and proceed to beat him up.

  • General: Ack. Just... ack. This guy is impossible to beat if you're below the recommended level, and even if you're at the recommended level he's tough. He's fast and heals a lot, so ack is a pretty good word for this situation.
  • Setup: Again, I'm pretty sure you don't feel like walking all the way back to Shadow Gulch to replenish your potions. Good thing you have some lying around.   • Defensive: He doesn't have any spells, so be thankful for that. He still hits pretty hard, though, so be careful of your team's health. Still, Velm should pull through for you.

Post-battle:

In another decidedly anticlimactic turn of events (wow, that was really flowery language), you don't bury the Grundo. You just talk to him. Anyway, equip those weapons and head west out of the cave (you're still on Hunting mode, right? That'll help.)

Once you leave the cave, start walking south on the path till the scenery changes. You'll know what I mean when you get there...

balthazar's grove

All this happy is slightly disturbing. Scratch that, very disturbing.

Yeah, it's all green... and happy... which is weird, considering the evil Chias I've been telling you about live pretty nearby. Anyhow, make sure you're on that mode I was telling you about earlier *cough* Hunting *cough*. If you look at the west end of the grove, you'll notice some faeries who don't look very cheery. That, of course, means they're bosses. Before we fight them, we have to grow to level 46/47/48.

As usual, there's a dungeon thingy we need to get through. It's not a dungeon, though, it's the Happy Fun Non-Haunted House. You're currently on Level 1. You need to get to the stairs to level 2, which will take you almost to the exit of the house. It's weird, yeah, but it's not like this section is normal. Keep Fighting as You Go till you get to the exit of the house. Try to gain a level in the process.

Once you get outside again, you'll notice the Brain Tree and a weird purple Bruce fella. He's actually the town, except rolled into one slightly anorexic package. Convenient. Take a look at your shopping list:

Shopping List

  • Milk
  • Eggs
  • Cheese
  • Potions:
    • 20 Sloth's Sloth Potions
    • 20 Corrode Potions
    • 10 Eyrie Flight Potions
  • Weapons/Armor:
    • The stuff for sale here is a decent jump up from the last town's stuff, and the next town isn't till next chapter. If someone's equipped weapon/armor doesn't pack bonuses, upgrade for them.
  • Inn: Hate to say it, but there's a (comparatively) high chance you'll die before the next chapter. So rest here. Gold probably isn't an issue for you anyway.

So yeah, don't hesitate to buy stuff, since there isn't another town for a while. Talk to the Brain Tree, too, he's got some useful info. If you aren't at level 46/47/48 yet, train in the house until you get to that level. Once you do, you can actually walk north from where the Bruce and the Tree are. That'll send you south (go figure) back into the Grove. Conveniently, you now know the secret word (whatever that is), so walk up to the faeries and prepare for a long, hard fight. (Don't you wish I had said that earlier?)

  • Strategy:
  • General: This is a weird boss fight since we've got 4 bosses at once. Also, these guys are a Really Tough Boss, so I'll go more in-depth in the strategy this time. (There are actually whole articles in the Neopian Times about how to beat these guys.) Basically, these guys can wreak a lot of havoc together. But if you pick them off one by one, the battle gets much easier.
  • Battle: I'll put all the info in a list for you:
    1. On their first turns, Mipsy and Velm should cast Group Haste and Group Shields respectively. Rohane and Talinia should toss Sloth's Sloth Potions at any Faerie on their first turns. Your team can use haste potions too if you like.
    2. Everyone should continue chucking slowing potions at the Faeries (doesn't matter what order) until all the faeries are slowed. Make sure you re-apply potions as necessary. If a faerie's slow wears off, chuck slow potions immediately until one works! (if you run out of Sloth's Sloth Potions, use some of the Vile's Apathy Potions.)
    3. Nice! You're done the setup. Velm should Group Heal absolutely every turn, and the others should attack just about every turn (Rohane should use melee, Mipsy should use Direct Damage, and Talinia can chuck damage potions or just attack melee. Her Multiple Targets is a nice skill to use too.)
    4. We're going to take out the faeries from least to most HP. First, everyone should aim at the Fire Faerie and keep beating her up until she dies. When she does, the faeries might not be slowed anymore. If so, take a quick break and re-slow the faeries. If your Group Shields or Group Haste has worn off, be sure to recast those.
    5. Good news! The battle's just gotten a whole lot easier. Everyone should now focus on taking out the Dark Faerie. Same deal as before. When she dies, re-apply the slow potions and Shield/Haste spells.
    6. Keep at it... now you want to take out the Water Faerie. Once she's dead, this battle is almost over. Slow the Earth Faerie and blast her until she's dead. Shouldn't take too long.

Post-battle:

Nice job! You've just beaten one of the hardest (if not THE hardest) bosses in the game. Collect your winnings, but don't go on. Switch to Normal mode (the baddies in the Grove don't give out much EXP now) and head back to the Haunted House (yeah, it changed its name.)

I'm not sure if you noticed, but when you enter the house there are gates on either side of the house, which let you get to the guys behind the house without actually having to go through it. (Before you ask, they were opened when you talked to the Brain Tree.) So walk on back and talk to the Bruce guy again. Restock your potions (you want 20 of the Sloth's Sloth and Corrode Potions) and sell your old weapons.

You can walk north from the guys again to get back into the Grove. Once you do, get ready for our next section...

land of the chias

See, I told you you'd get to beat up Chias eventually.

Walk over to where the Faeries were (you'll notice everything looks all evil again) and start going west. Look at your map for Hubrid's Mountain Range or something to that effect. Stay on Normal mode and head through the woods and mountains until you find yourself in the Esophagor's Swamp or the Goo Bog. Don't fight anyone or enter the fortress. Let me repeat that:

Don't touch anything!

Why? Well, the baddies around here are called evil for a reason: they're ridiculously strong and attack in droves. One baddie, the Hand of Nox, can Obliterate Mipsy in two shots if she's a little hurt. Mipsy's died plenty of times against these guys on my watch. The Heart of Nox uses the odd Focus Energy ability to become just about indestructible. And the other Chias aren't much easier.

But don't worry. We'll be back soon. And yeah, we'll enter that fortress and beat up the leader of these Chias.

Big Important Note

Don't spend those skill points...

Pretty soon you'll reach level 50, which is a Really Big Deal. Once you grow to level 50, spend that skill point, but when you reach level 51, don't spend that skill point! We're actually going to save our skill points until we reach level 60, at which point we'll spend them.

I know what you're thinking: why? Is it cause I feel like it? Well, partially, but not really. See, weapons and armor in Faerieland (act 5) give big bonuses. I'm talking 2-3 points for 3 or more skills. Well, take a look at Tricia and Arthur.

Tricia is your standard, no-nonsense NQII player. She figured it just makes sense to spend your skill points as soon as you get them. So every time she levels up in Act 5, she spends the skill point she gets immediately. By level 60, Rohane has 15 points in Critical Attacks and Damage Increase, so those stats are maxed out.

But at level 60, she finds the Sword of Apocalypse, which gives 2 points bonus in all of Rohane's non-innate skills. Rohane's Critical Attacks and Damage Increase would go up, but they can't. So she's just wasted 4 skill points, which is a Really Big Deal. And it isn't a good idea to have wasted skill points when you're up against the toughest boss in the game.

Arthur decides to try the save-your-skill-points method, because he listened to me and I'm *ahem* awesome like that. So he stops spending skill points after level 50. He spends his skill point when he grew to level 50, but after that he saves his points until he gets to level 60. At level 60, his Rohane only has 13 points in Damage Increase and Critical Attacks, so he doesn't have the easiest time in Faerieland.

But his patience is rewarded. At level 60, he finds the Sword of Apocalypse, which gives 2 points bonus in all of Rohane's non-innate skills. Rohane's Critical Attacks and Damage Increase go to 15. Bam, no skill points wasted, it just falls neatly into place.

So now, Arthur and Tricia are on the same level, skill-wise. The only difference is that Arthur has 10 more skill points to spend, which is, again, a Really Big Deal. The last boss is so much easier for him now. Thank you, planning ahead.

The gist of it is that you should stop spending skill points once you reach level 50. You can spend those saved skill points after the 2nd-to-last-boss. I'll let you know when it's time. And now back to your regularly scheduled production.

goo bog

He sure looks like a fun guy. Haha... fungus humor.

Yeah, instead of training in the home of the barbaric Chias, we're going to train a bit here in the Goo Bog (your map might call it the Esophagor's Swamp or something like that.) Go ahead and beat up the fungi and other slime thingies that live here. Go far enough into the swamp that you see scattered trees but not too far. If you look at your map you'll see what I mean.

You'll want to train to level 49/50/51 (yeah, 3 levels) here. Don't worry, it isn't that bad. The baddies here give incredible experience and they're far easier than those Chias I told you about earlier. So what's not to like?

Now that that's done, we can finally go back to Chia-land. Before you leave the Goo Bog, though, Switch to Normal mode. No point fighting the Chias anymore, since they don't give out much experience. Well, now head northwest out of the Goo Bog until you're in the mountain range you came through. Remember the tower in the middle of the mountains? (If not, open up your map of the Mountain Range.) Yeah, that's where we're headed.

Take a look at your map for Nox's Fortress, Level 1. Yeah, there are 9 levels of this thing. I trust you can make your way up through the 9 levels. If you run into any Chias, feel free to kill 'em. Once you make it to the top of the tower (Level 9), you'll notice an especially big Chia, who's incidentally your next boss.

hubrid nox

  • Strategy:
  • General: This is, hands down, the most annoying boss in the whole game. Not tough, just annoying, mostly because he heals. A lot. See, he's insanely fast and can sometimes get two turns in a row. Combine that with his healing power and he's a tough cookie.
  • Setup: Make sure this guy is slowed all the time. Toss those Vile's Apathy Potions from Shadow Gulch as soon as the battle starts, and make sure you throw more if his slow wears off. If you run out of those potions, use the ones from the Lost Desert. Also make sure you use Velm's Group Shields and Mipsy's Group Haste, they'll be really useful. I'd recommend that Rohane uses some haste potions (he's your best attacker this battle), and if you have time the others can use them too.
  • Offensive: You really must do as much damage as possible to this guy, or else he'll just heal it all off. Rohane and Mipsy should use their standard attacks, while Talinia should toss Rot or Blister Potions (her melee is nice, though, since she's able to get a Shockwave off.) If Velm gets a turn when he doesn't need to heal, have him toss damage potions too. Rohane's Stunning Strikes will be really useful this battle.
  • Defensive: As if taking Nox down wasn't hard enough, he hits pretty hard too. Velm should always have Group Shields up (he should have them up every battle) and should heal whenever someone is hurt. If Nox gets two turns in a row and decides to Obliterate Mipsy... yeah, not fun. Velm can use haste potions too, they'll be useful.

Post-battle:

Once you finally kill this guy off, give yourself a pat on the back. You won't have to face another boss as hard as this guy for... maybe 5 more levels. I know it isn't much, but hey.

You're really close to the end of this chapter. Take the teleporter outside the fortress, and head back to the Goo Bog. Grow to level 50/51/52 in the Goo Bog, then use your maps to get to the Esophagor in the southeast corner of the swamp. Walk on up to him and get ready to fight him.

Nice! You've just finished Act 4. All that's left is Act 5, where you'll find misguided flying cats and the huge ugly dragon at the end of the game. Head on over to ThePhoenixRising's page to get started. See you next page.
