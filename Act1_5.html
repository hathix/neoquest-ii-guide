hallo

Hey, you're back! Looks like you've just beaten the big red elephant dude, Zombom. (If you haven't beaten him yet, go finish the Act 1, Part 1 walkthrough.) Now you're ready to move on to the secret second half of Act 1. From now on, come back to this page instead of Epanastasi's. You can get here by clicking on Act 1.5 in the header.

Before we start... I've got good news for you. By beating Zombom, you've gotten farther than 90% of all NQIIers ever have. So give yourself a pat on the back, you're turning pro fast.

Oh yeah, the game. I forgot.

You've got Mipsy

Don't be fooled. She's just itching to Obliterate you.

So you're back in White River. It doesn't seem like anything's changed, huh? Well, walk south from where you entered town. Looks like the construction workers stopped being lazy the bridge magically came down. Keep walking south a bit till you see a house to your left. Pop inside and... hey! There's Mipsy! Talk to her and ask her to join your team. She's cool like that so she will.

You're probably ready to go out there and start beating stuff up with your new wizard pal, right? Hold your horses, eager beaver. You've got to spend her skill points first. Just take a look at the conveniently-placed table below.

On this difficulty... Spend Mipsy's skill points here.
Normal 6 points in Direct Damage
5 points in Innate Melee Defense
Evil 7 points in Direct Damage
5 points in Innate Melee Defense
Insane 8 points in Direct Damage
5 points in Innate Melee Defense

Now that you have your spunky new character, let's do some shopping. Head across the street to Sildairm's shop and buy 5 Awakening Potions. If someone dies from here on out, you'll have these to revive them. And yeah, they're expensive. But you'll live. (*insert boos at bad pun here*)If Zombom gave you a sword, buy new armor and sell your old armor. If he gave you armor, don't buy anything cause the sword for sale here is the same as the one you have already. Now leave town to the south.

Let's test drive our vicious new friend now. Switch to Hunting mode and walk around a bit. Remember the Advanced Battling Guide? Yeah, you'll want to reread that now that you've got friends with you. Baddies will start coming in pairs soon, so Mipsy should hammer stuff while Rohane finishes 'em off. Test out your new battling style on the poor little Lupe dudes here.

Well, once that's finished we can move on. Switch back to Normal mode (go ahead and beat up any baddies you run into) and open your map site to Southern Meridell. See that lake surrounded by hills/trees in the southwesternish part of the map? We wanna go there. You're currently around the very top of the map. Walk into the swamp first, then go southwestish from there. You'll see the Eye of Meridell (that's the lake thingy) soon. The town of Lakeside is, well, right next to the lake. You'll find it.

lakeside

Another name in the long line of uber-creative NQII names.

Despite the name, Lakeside is a pretty cool place. When you enter, open up your map to Lakeside's map. Yeah, it's a pretty big place. The first place you want to stop at is the inn, the first building on your left. Talk to the Uni and ask to stay at the inn.

After that, exit the inn and look for the weapons/armor shop in the middle of town. There are two Zafaras called Zapka and Pipka there. Walk over to that shop and talk to the pink guy. Buy a sword from him, equip it, and sell your old sword. Never buy wands for Mipsy. Walk over and talk to the blue guy now. Buy armor for Rohane here (you know what to do with your old armor, right?) No need to buy robes for Mipsy since the robes here only give a 2 point increase over the ones she has now.

Just south of the weapons/armor building is the tavern, aka potion shop. Party time! YEAH! Talk to the green Scorchio dude and buy 5 Flame Potions and 5 Dawdling Potions. No, Mipsy, we can't buy anything else. Now take a look at your map again and find Potraddo, the hermit in the northwest corner of town. Hike over to him and ask him about some lost city you apparently know all about. Once that's done, we can leave town. Yeah, you have to walk back to the entrance. Tough beans.

Once you're out of town, switch to Hunting mode and train here till both Rohane and Mipsy are at level 13/14/15. Once you level up, look at your buddy the skills table (he hasn't moved from the right side of the screen.) He hasn't changed much, but remember you have to spend points for two peeps now.

Open your map to Southern Meridell again and look for the desert to your east. Walk over there on Normal mode. Resist the urge to attack stuff... for now at least. Go southwest through the desert until you see a ruined city thingy. Don't enter that just yet. Instead, keep going southwest until you see a grassy patch near the coast.

This is an no-encounter zone, which means you won't run into any monsters here. This way, you can walk onto the desert to pick a fight and then walk around on the grass after each battle to get absolutely free HP. So go ahead and train to level 14/15/16 here. If you get badly hurt, have Rohane use Healing Potions and Mipsy use Flasks.

Once you level up, look for that ruined city again. Except this time, enter it.

the lost city of phorofor

Better to lose a city than lose your mind, I always say.

Once you enter town, go southeast a few steps and talk to the cynical ghost guy guarding the entrance. Conveniently, he'll disappear once you talk to him. Open up your map site to The Lost City of Phorofor and look for the Giant Sand Grundo in the southeast corner of town. Switch to Hunting mode and start walking, beating up baddies as you go along. Oh great, I smell another word from our sponsors...

The NEW Fight-As-You-Go Method!

Bored of training? Fed up with running from every monster you run into? Well, thanks to the patented new Fight-As-You-Go Method, you don't have to anymore!

That's right, there's a solution! Just fight as you go!

All you need to do is switch to Hunting mode! Then start walking and beating up monsters as you go!

Enjoy your walk and gain experience as you go! So what are you waiting for?!?!?

**Brought to you by the NQII Help Club!**

DISCLAIMER: Method does not work if you are eaten by Chias while walking. And if you're wondering, no, we're not affiliated with those Chias. And stop reading small print, it's bad for your eyes.

Yeah. As I was saying, look at your map. Find the Giant Sand Grundo and walk over to him using that funky new Fight-As-You-Go method. If you're hurt, use Healing Potions or Flasks. Before you fight him, get to level 15/16/17 (if you weren't there already.)

giant sand grundo

More like giant sumo grundo.
Recommended Level: 15 on Normal
16 on Evil
17 on Insane
HP: 130 on Normal
162 on Evil
195 on Insane
Special Abilities: Critical Attacks
Prizes: 1 weapon/armor
300 NP
  • Strategy:
  • General: Compared to Zombom, this dude's a walk in the park. He may not smell very good but he's an easy boss to beat. Mipsy's magic should have him gone in no time.
  • Offensive: Mipsy should, obviously, use her Direct Damage attacks. Rohane can use Flame Potions if you really want to, but this guy isn't going to last very long as is.
  • Defensive: This fat guy won't have many chances to hit you, but he does hit pretty hard. Don't be afraid to use healing potions if you're hurt.

Post-Battle:

You know what to do. Equip what you just got and step into the conveniently-placed teleporter. Open up your map site to Tower On A Hill, Level 2. Use the Fight-As-You-Go method and make your way through the 2 levels of the tower, healing as you need to. Get to level 16/17/18 before you leave.

Once you're out of there, switch to Normal mode and open up your map to Southeast Meridell. You just came out of the tower (duh) and you need to get to Seaside on the coast. Walk on over there, beating up any baddies you run into. Step on Seaside once you get there.

seaside

Avast, landlubber! Knit me a sweater or ye'll walk the plank!

This creatively-named town has a good bit of stuff in it. First things first, open up your map to this town's page (I trust you can do that.) Enter the first building to your north and talk to the guy there, but don't buy anything. Now look for the guy in the northeast corner of town. Walk up to him and talk to him. Tell him you're sorry about something or other, and he'll tell you that his bro (the guy in that shop you just visited) will give you a discount. *woohoo*

Go back to the store and buy 10 Blast and Dawdling Potions. If you like you can grab a few haste potions as well. Now look for Patannis, the dude in the southwestern building. Buy a sword and armor for Rohane here. If the Sand Grundo didn't give you a robe for Mipsy, buy one here. Sell your old weapons/armor and leave town.

Switch to Hunting mode and train here till you're level 17/18/19. When you reach that level, switch back to Normal and open up your map to the Plains of Retreat. Walk over to Meridell Castle.

This place is home to a bunch of jerks, it seems, cause no one will talk to you. Oh well. Open up your map to the Meridell Castle page. You're at the only entrance (that's in the east, if you were wondering) and your boss is in the north room. Walk over to this Ramtor guy and pick a fight with him.

ramtor, first time

Well, at least he isn't fat.
Recommended Level: 17 on Normal
18 on Evil
19 on Insane
HP: 200 on Normal
250 on Evil
300 on Insane
Special Abilities: Liquefy - 50HP Direct Damage
Zeal - 18% Haste
Repair - 70HP Heal
Entice - Mesmerization
Prizes: None. Muhaha.
  • Strategy:
  • General: Don't be scared by Ramtor's big goodie bag, cause he'll flee once you bring his HP down to 100 or so. Toss a slow potion to start if you like (not necessary though), then just go on the offensive and he should run back to his mommy in no time.
  • Offensive: Mipsy's magic should make Ramtor cry at this point, so go ahead and use that. Rohane should just swing his sword around, since you'll want to save those damage potions for later.
  • Defensive: Ramtor won't get many chances to attack, but you should stil be careful. If he's lucky, he can kill Mipsy in two shots with Liquefy. So have Mipsy heal if her HP falls below 50.

Post-Battle:

As I said, Ramtor will run away when his HP goes down to about 100. As I didn't say, the guys in the castle will start talking to you now. Look for Guard Thyet on your map... he's the guy just north of where you enter. If you talk to him he'll heal you up for free. For once, I'm serious. There may be no such thing as a free lunch, but there is such a thing as a free inn.

southern meridell

OK, so maybe this section is randomly placed. Whaddya gonna do, throw tomatoes at me? *ducks*

Now that that spineless Bruce/Eyrie/thingamajig Ramtor fellow has run away, you probably want to hunt him down. I don't blame you, but you'll have to gain a level here before you do. Exit the castle and head west from it. Keep going west until you run into some hills. Train to level 18/19/20 here. If you're hurt, go back to Thyet in the castle to heal. Just step anywhere on the castle to get warped to the entrance.

Once you've leveled up, open up your map back up to the Plains of Retreat. Switch to Normal mode and walk northwestish toward the tower in the west of the map. Just for practice (and for fun... cause who doesn't like beating stuff up?), beat up any baddies you run into. Step on the tower when you get there.

ramtor's tower

Another boss with a tower? Why can't I get one?!?

Open up your map to Ramtor's Tower: Base Level. (In case you're wondering, no, I have no idea why the NQII designers put a force field in the game but people still fight with swords. If I was Rohane I'd whip out my pocket blaster and start blasting those baddies' pockets off. Just me though.) At any rate, you're at the grassy foyer thingy and you need to get to the stairs in the southwest corner. We're going to use the Fight-As-You-Go method during this tower, so you know what to do: switch to Hunting mode and beat up anything that comes in your way. Climb the stairs when you get to them.

So now you're on Dungeon Level 1 of the tower. Open up your map to that level. You just climbed the stairs in the southwest, and you need to get to the stairs in the northwest. Make your way through the level, continuing to beat up baddies. Before you climb those stairs at the end of the level, make sure you're at least at level 19/20/21.

Now you're back on the Base Level, so open your map to that page. As you might expect, you're at the stairs in the northwest. Train around here till you're level 20/21/22 (in case you forgot, you can still hover your mouse over the 20/21/22 to see more info.) The demonic grarrls here get on my nerves a lot since they like to pick on Mipsy and hit pretty hard. So just flee from them and pick on the wimpier Chias (not diabolical, thankfully) and Meercas. Once that's done, switch to Normal mode and walk over to the other set of stairs.

Once you've climbed those stairs, you'll be in the Tower level of this tower. (Don't ask...) Remember those demonic grarrls? Yeah. They attack in pairs now. Not pretty. So just flee from them... don't bother fighting them, it's not worth it. Anyhow, make your way to the southwest corner of this level... oi! It's that dude I beat up a little while ago! Heal up and then attack him.

ramtor, second time

Cool mustache, but that robe? Ew. He's gotta go.
Recommended Level: 20 on Normal
21 on Evil
22 on Insane
HP: 200 on Normal
250 on Evil
300 on Insane
Special Abilities: Liquefy - 50HP Direct Damage
Zeal - 18% Haste
Repair - 70HP Heal
Entice - Mesmerize
Prizes: 2 weapons/armor
500 NP
  • Strategy:
  • General: "Hey, he only has 100 HP left after that... encounter, right? Easy!"
    Well, not really. He healed himself up all the way. But hey, that's just more suffering for him. This fight should be a lot like the last one, except he'll use magic more. Throw Dawdling Potions until one works, then start attacking until he dies.
  • Offensive: Mipsy's magic still hurts. A lot. So have her smack Ramtor in the face whenever she can. Rohane should just attack normally. When the Dawdling Potion wears off (you'll know when the "Slow" disappears from below Ramtor's name) have Rohane toss more until one works (you'll see "Slow" appear under Ramtor's name.)
  • Defensive: Same thing as before: he'll try to Liquefy his stocks Mipsy. Make sure Mipsy uses a Healing Bottle if her HP falls below 50.

Post-Battle:

Once Ramtor's toast, give yourself a pat on the back. You've just graduated from this chapter and (more importantly) this page. You can now move on to the Act 2 Walkthrough on CmdrInputBlocker's page. So, uh, bye until then.
