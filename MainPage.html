Game Interface

You can't learn anything about NQII unless you know the basics. Learning how to fight before learning about the interface is like learning how to ride a horse without knowing what a horse is. (OK, that was a bad comparison, but you get the gist.)

So when you start off your NQII game (you'll want to do that so you can follow along), you'll come across a screen like this:

Except all the numbers, of course, but that sort of stuff goes without saying. There's loads of information on the screen, so I've split it up into 3 pieces. As you read about each button/link/doohickey, click it in your game to see for yourself what it does.

Interface Part 1: The Top Bar

Basically, all the info on your team goes up here. Really, don't worry about Health or Experience. We'll talk more about it later. After you've looked at this picture, take a look at the top bar in your game.

Interface Part 2: The Right Bar

This is probably the most important bar. You can do all sorts of stuff here, including accessing your items and moving around. Don't feel overwhelmed by the sheer amount of stuff... really, it's not that bad. I think I should clam up now and let the picture do the talking.

Actually, I have one quick thing to say first. After you look at the picture, click some of the links in your game. Try moving around a few steps. See your inventory or talk to mom if you like. Try to get to know the bar. You'll be great friends, trust me.

Interface Part 3: The Map

The map isn't way too important because you can't really click anything on it. Take a look at the picture to get familiar with the map.

items and your inventory

Unless you want to become Chia food, you need to learn how to use items. Items are awesome: you can heal yourself, equip yourself with stuff, and... well, that's actually it, but you get the gist of it.

shopping

Now you know all about items. That's cool and all, but how exactly do you get these so-called item thingies? Well, you buy them from merchants. And you can also sell items if you want to. Here you'll learn all about shopping.

In order to shop, you need to walk up to a merchant and select Trade with [name]. Walk over to Pongoras (he's the Ixi in the green shirt) and ask to trade with him. That'll give you a taste of what the shopping interface looks like.

buying stuff

At the top of every "shopping page" there's an option to trade with the merchant. This is how you buy potions and weapons. I think you'll learn more from the picture than from me:

selling stuff

Now, buying stuff is easy, since you do it in real life. But when's the last time you sold a sword to a shopkeeper? I thought so. But still, it's easy. Scroll down from the buying menu and you'll see the selling menu. And quick clarification: sell all your unequipped weapons, except for the Father's sword. You'll see why at the end of the game.

skills guide

Skills are what make the world go round. Well, not really. More accurately, they make battling a whole lot easier. Once you know all about skills, you can blast stuff in the face with damage spells, stun stuff with your flashy sword, and more. So what's not to love?

Before you start, though, there are a few things you need to know.

  • You gain skill points when you level up.
  • You spend skill points on these skills.
  • You can spend up to 15 skill points in any skill.
  • Skills get better as you spend more skill points in them.

Well, now that you've read that, you can skip the actual skills if you want. They're good reference for later, though, if you're into that sort of thing.

rohane
Critical Attacks If you invest here, Rohane can occasionally hit baddies extra hard. The more points you invest here, the more often he'll score a critical hit. (If you extend it to its max level, Rohane can score a critical hit nearly every other turn.)
9. Great skill to have, since who doesn't like dealing more damage? The only problem is that it isn't that consistent.
Damage Increase As you can imagine, this skill allows Rohane to deal more damage whenever he swings his sword around.
10. More damage = win. What more can I say?
Combat Focus If this skill is activated, Rohane gets in the zone, allowing him to take less damage from melee hits. Unfortunately he also deals less damage.
1. Rohane's pretty fat resistant as is, so he can take plenty of hits normally. Besides, do you really want to reduce his hitting power? Didn't think so.
Stunning Strikes If you invest here, Rohane can occasionally stun someone he hits, which basically sets back their next turn a few seconds. The more points you put here, the more often he'll stun and the longer the stun will last.
7. Nifty skill, especially in the beginning of the game, where potions are a Really Big Deal. Later, though, baddies will start resisting it, and you won't need to stun stuff as much anyway.
Battle Taunt When activated, Rohane starts making loud "Yo Mama" jokes. This, of course, encourages baddies to target him instead of other characters.
3. This skill is useful on paper, but I hardly ever find myself using it except against the final boss. It's really useful during that battle, though.
Innate Magic Resistance This skill makes Rohane's skull thicker (is that even possible?) As a result, baddies' magic doesn't hurt him as much. The more points you put here, the less damage magic does.
3. As I said, Rohane's pretty fat solid so he can afford to take magic attacks. Our points are better spent elsewhere.
Innate Melee Haste With this skill, the time in between turns for him decreases. (i.e. he moves faster.) The more points you put here, the faster he moves.
9. Awesome skill. The faster Rohane goes, the faster he'll be able to put the hurt on baddies.
mipsy
Direct Damage When Mipsy casts this spell, she hurts 1 enemy for a lot of HP. At its max level, this can deal 100 damage.
10. This is Mipsy's best way of dealing damage. This skill is so awesome that it just lets you breeze by the second half of Act 1.
Group Direct Damage When Mipsy casts this spell, she hurts all enemies, but for less damage than Direct Damage. At its max level, this can deal, 64 damage to each baddie. Also, this skill takes more time to execute than Direct Damage.
5. Eh, it's an OK skill, but Direct Damage is way better. This skill is a lot worse against bosses, plus it's pretty slow. Mipsy isn't the fastest girl in the world so she can't afford to use such a slow spell.
Group Haste When Mipsy casts this spell, everyone on your team gets temporarily hasted a bit (it's like you increase their Innate Haste.) At max level, everyone is hasted for 38% for about 30 seconds.
7. Neat skill. Without it, you'd have to have everyone use hasting potions during each boss battle, which would take really long. The only problem with this skill is that it isn't that useful until it's at level 8 or so.
Slowing When Mipsy casts this spell, one enemy is slowed, so it takes more time for them to do stuff. (This is a lot like tossing a slowing potion.) At max level, Mipsy can slow baddies up to 62%.
2. Fun, but not all that useful. Slowing potions were made for a reason, you know. Mipsy has better places to spend her skill points.
Damage Shields Mipsy uses the Force to make a wall around your team, which hurts anyone who melee attacks you. Baddies who use magic don't get hurt, though. At max level, baddies get hurt for 22 HP each time they hit your team.
3. This skill hardly does any damage, which is bad for it since Mipsy can deal 100 damage in one shot if she wants to. This skill is defensive, and Mipy's offensive. Again, Mipsy has better places to spend her skills points.
Innate Melee Defense If you invest here, Mipsy invests in uglier thicker robes, which makes her get hurt less by baddies.
10. This is a mandatory skill for Mipsy. Normally, she's about as sturdy as a wet paper towel, so without this skill, Mipsy becomes Chia bait. Not pretty.
Innate Casting Haste This skill is a lot like everyone else's Innate Haste. If you invest here, her time between turns goes down.
9. With this skill, Mipsy can inflict pain and suffering more often. That's pretty awesome.
talinia
Increased Bow Damage I'm pretty sure you know what this skill does. Talinia deals more damage whenever she sticks someone with an arrow.
10. More damage = win. Especially for Talinia, who isn't that strong by default.
Multiple Targets Occasionally, Talinia will be able to shoot more than 1 arrow at a time, so this skill works a bit like Mipsy's Group Damage. At level 1, she can shoot 2 arrows at a time. At level 6, she can shoot 3, and at level 11 she can shoot 4.
7. Pretty useful skill, despite its inconsistency. Talinia can really deal out pain when she shoots 4 arrows at a time.
Ranged Attacks Whenever someone tries to hit Talinia, their turn gets set back a bit. Basically, anyone who hits Talinia gets stunned.
6. This skill is good because baddies are stunned every time they hit Talinia. The problem is that they don't always aim at Talinia. Also, I think the next skill is a bit more useful than this one.
Shockwave Imagine Rohane's Stunning Strikes. Now imagine all enemies getting stunned instead of just one. Yup, occasionally Talinia will be able to stun all enemies when she hits any one of them.
8. This is a great ability, since if all baddies are stunned you gain a lot of free time. The only problem with Shockwave is that it gets resisted (i.e. it doesn't go off as much) toward the end of the game.
Slowing Strikes Each time Talinia hits a baddie, they get slowed a little (this slowing is cumulative with slowing from potions/spells.) The slowing wears off quickly, though. At the max level, baddies are slowed 5% whenever Talinia hits them.
3. 5% is nice as an interest rate but it just isn't enough in NeoQuest II. Talinia has better places to spend her skill points.
Innate Magic Resistance If you invest here, Talinia gives baddies the evil eye (she's good at it), so their magic is less effective.
3. Same as with Rohane. Talinia doesn't have quite as much HP, but Velm arrives fairly soon after you get Talinia, so she won't need to worry about getting hurt that much. You can spend points elsewhere.
Innate Melee Haste If you spend points here, Talinia buys some 4-Hour-Energy, which helps reduce the time between turns for her (or, she just moves faster.)
9. If you haven't noticed the theme already, haste is really important. With more speed, Talinia can effectively deal more damage. That's pretty nice.
velm
Healing When Velm casts this spell, he heals one team member (sorta like that person using a healing potion.) At max level, he can heal someone for 150 HP. (Fun fact: in order for him to heal someone, you need to click on someone's picture during battle.)
6. I mean, it's nice, but we have Healing Potions for a reason. Besides, chances are you'll have more than one person getting hurt at a time, so Healing won't work as well. And also, I'm too lazy to scroll down and click on a character every time I want to heal them.
Group Healing When Velm casts this, he heals everyone on the team. The catch is that you don't heal for as much as Healing, and this spell is slower than Healing. At max level, this heals everyone for 90 HP.
10. I like this skill more than Healing. This skill is really convenient and pretty effective, since most of the time you have 2 or 3 people hurt. This is the reason why Velm makes the game so much easier.
Group Shielding Velm too can use the Force. When he casts this, he increases the Magic Restistance and Defense of all your team members. That means both melee attacks and magic do less damage than normal.
9. Another incredibly useful skill. With the ability to shield and heal, Velm can make your team near-indestructible.
Mesmerization When Velm casts this on an enemy, the enemy is basically stunned for a really long time. The only catch is that the enemy snaps out of it if they're either hit or healed. So basically, you Mesmerize someone and then ignore them for a while.
7. When Velm isn't healing, this is a good way for him to spend his turn. Mes is like a free stun, which is always nice. The problem is that it gets resisted a lot toward the end of the game.
Celestial Hammer Yeah, this is the thing Velm uses to hit Chias. This is basically a spell that both hurts and stuns baddies. At its max level, it can deal 45 damage and stun for 3 seconds.
4. Very fun skill, but it's not all that useful. The damage is hardly anything, and the stun is cool but Mesmerization does it way better. Velm's just not made to go on the offensive.
Innate Melee Defense As you might expect, this skill causes Velm to take less damage when baddies attack him.
8. Velm can heal, sure, but he needs his defense. You don't want our amnesiac friend to get mauled, now do you? Didn't think so.
Innate Casting Haste Velm steals some 4-Hour-Energy from Talinia and uses that to decrease the time between his turns (that just means he goes faster.)
10. Velm's not that fast, and healing spells aren't that fast either. Unless you want your team to get eaten, make sure you put plenty of points here.

one last thing

OK, so you know how to beat stuff up. That's good and all, but knowing how to use a hammer doesn't help you make a sculpture, if you'll excuse that horrible metaphor. Before I send you out into the big scary overworld, you have to know the best way to battle.

I think this picture really says it all.

Just beat up one baddie till it's dead, at which point focus on another baddie. Keep beating up baddies until they're all dead. It really doesn't matter what order you kill baddies in. Some people go left to right, some (including me) go highest HP to lowest HP... your call.
