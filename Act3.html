bonjour

Hiya! If you haven't noticed already, you're at the Act 3 Walkthrough. I'm pretty sure you know what I'm going to say but I'll say it anyway. If you haven't finished Act 2 yet, go and finish that. Also, go ahead and bookmark this page if you like, since you'll be coming back here for a while.

Nothing much more to add here. You might be interested to know that you're 50% through the game (at least level-wise) and you only have 5 more tough levels ahead of you. After that, the game becomes pretty easy. There are no Chias in this chapter, but there are plenty of undead things, if that helps.

Before you start, take a look at your old friend the skills table. He's having serious weight problems, so I've had to abbreviate some of the skill/character names (Talinia's Increased Bow Damage has become Incrsd. Bow Dmg.) Don't worry, you can still hover your mouse over the skill name abbreviations to see the whole thing.

Also, about your map site: make sure you navigate to the Lost Desert page before looking for any maps. You've done this sort of thing before.

Anyway, on to the guide. Have fun, and don't get eaten.

sakhmet city

Wait, we're in a giant dessert?!? Awesome! No wait, it's a desert...

Well, Sakhmet City isn't the coolest of places, but it sure beats a town full of Chias. You can talk to the guys next to you if you want, but you really don't have to. Head north and west to leave the building. Now you're out in the big scary world again. See those tents to your west? They're the actual town. There are three tents you have to be concerned with:

  • Inn - Yellow tent in northeast
  • Potions - Yellow tent in southwest
  • Weapons/Armor - Light blue tent in southeast (the one with the sword on it)

I'm pretty sure you know what to do when you're in a town by now. Where's that Shopping List?

Shopping List

  • Milk
  • Eggs
  • Cheese
  • Potions: Note to self: these'll come in handy later.
    • 20 Scorch Potions
    • 10 Potions of Loafing Laziness
    • 5 Potions of Great Haste
  • Weapons/Armor:
    • Note to self: only buy if the thing for sale has 5 or more points than what you already have. For example, the bow here has 25 power. Only buy it if Talinia's current bow has less than 20 power.
  • Inn: No need to rest here now.

If you're wondering what I meant under Weapons/Armor, I'm saying that you should be careful about buying weapons/armor from stores now. From here on out, the weapons/armor dropped by bosses will have some pretty nifty bonuses. Unless the weapon you have right now is much weaker than the one for sale, don't buy.

If you're still confused, Bonuses beat Power.

Enough about that. Now that you're done shopping, it's time to hit the road sand. We need to get training. Go a little north from the huts and start training. We need to get to level 32/33/34 - that's right, two levels. If you're hurt badly (i.e. someone's health is yellow), run back to the inn (that's the yellow northeastern tent) and rest. Why? We need to conserve potions. Besides, cash probably isn't an issue anymore.

Once you've gained those two levels, open up your map to South Lost Desert. See the Temple of the Sky over to your northeast? Walk on over there and enter once you get there.

temple of the sky

So let me get this straight... the Temple of the Sky is on the ground. Yup, the NQII designers were geniuses, all right.

Another dungeon. Ew. We'll have to get used to it, though, since there are 3 more dungeon-ish things in this chapter. Oh well. You're currently on Level 1. If you glance at your map, this floor looks pretty big. Nonsense. You just have to walk north to the grassy area and then go west. We're going to use the Fight-As-You-Go method throughout this entire temple (we need to gain a level by the end of the next floor. Don't worry about leveling up on this floor.) So, uh, have fun walking and fighting through this level.

Now you're on Level 2. It's a bit of a maze but, hey, you've got maps, so you'll be able to figure it out. Keep using that Fight-As-You-Go method until you grow to level 33/34/35. Once you gain level up, switch to Normal mode and make your way through the rest of the level. Eventually you'll come across your next boss. Heal up and get ready to face him...

siliclast

What, is it too much work to smile?

  • Strategy:
  • General: This big rock dude is nothing special as far as bosses go, but he's still pretty tough. He can hit pretty hard and he has decent HP (although he can't heal.) With a bit of luck you should be able to beat him.
  • Setup: First off, make sure he's slowed at all times. Start the battle by having everyone chuck slowing potions until one works. When the slowing potion wears off, toss another one. (That goes without saying.) Rohane will be your main damage dealer, so he should use a hasting potion early in the battle.
  • Offensive: As I was saying, Rohane will be your main damage dealer. He should focus on using his melee attack. As you might expect, Mipsy should toss around Direct Damage spells. Talinia isn't that hard of a hitter yet, so she should throw Scorch Potions.
  • Defensive: This guy's melee is pretty powerful,and his Group Damage hurts too. Watch everyone's health and make sure they heal if their HP goes yellow. Be very careful with Mipsy, since Siliclast can kill her in 2 hits once she's down to yellow.

Post-battle:

Keep throwing stuff at Siliclast and he'll die, as you might expect. There's a conveniently-placed teleporter behind where Siliclast was, so step on it. You're back in Sakhmet City. (I really have to get one of those teleporters.) Head out to town and replenish (that's fancy talk for restock) the slowing/damage potions you used. (The potions shop is the yellow southwestern tent.) Of course, sell your old weapons.

Open your map back up to South Lost Desert. You'll want to walk over to the Ruined Temple all the way in the north. Once you get there, enter the temple.

ruined temple

The NQII developers were really creative with names, I'm telling you.

If you look at the maps for this place, you'll notice that it's 4 stories high and not really ruined at all. Only Level 1 is ruined. Speaking of which, you're on that level. We have to gain a level of our own before we take on the boss at the top of this temples, so we'll be using the Fight-As-You-Go method again. (Once you level up, switch back to Normal mode.) Go ahead and make your way through this level of the temple.

You're now on Level 2. This floor is unremarkable except for the conference room in the southeast. (I'm serious, look at the map. Doesn't it look like a conference room?) Just walk through this level.

Level 3 is slightly trickier. You start at the stairs in the northeast, and you have to get to the stairs in the northwest. Walk through this level too. (Chances are you'll have leveled up by now. If you have, remember to switch back to Normal mode. If you haven't leveled up yet, train on this level until you level up.)

Now you're on Level 4. Your next boss is in the very south of this room. Walk on over to him, heal up, and take him on.

gebarn ii

  • Strategy:
  • General: This undead Krawk has a big goodie bag and isn't afraid to use it. However, once you beat him you'll get Velm, which is awesome. Go ahead and beat this guy up.
  • Setup: You want him moving as slow as possible. Toss some Potions of Loafing Laziness first until he's slowed, and re-throw them when one wears off. If you like, your guys can use hasting potions. They'll come in handy.
  • Offensive: Gebarn hits hard, so we need to dispose of him as quickly as we can. Mipsy should use Direct Damage, as always. Rohane and Talinia should throw Scorch Potions (they'll be your best way of dealing damage.) If you run out of Scorch Potions, just use melee.
  • Defensive: Good news: after this battle, you probably won't need healing potions any more, so don't be afraid to heal. Watch everyone's HP and make sure they heal if their HP bar becomes yellow/red.

Post-battle:

Once he's dead, you can start partying: the game will soon become about 3 times easier. But first, take the teleporter behind where Gebarn stood and... you'll be back in Sakhmet. How convenient. Head out to the yellow southwestern tent to replenish the potions you used.

Leave town to the north and make sure you're on Normal mode (the baddies around here are jokes now, and not very funny ones either.) Head back north to the Ruined Temple. If you noticed, the mountains on either side of the temple are gone, allowing you to go farther north. Go north and you'll see a village nearby.

waset village

Eat your vegetables!

Say hi to this new village made of 4 tents. As exciting as buying potions is, we've got more important things to do. Enter the yellow tent. That's the inn. But more importantly, you've got a Techo to chat with. Walk over to the east side of the inn and talk to the guy there. Guess who?

Yup, it's Velm. Ask him to join you, and he will. Congrats! The best NQII character (in my opinion) has just joined your team. Before we shock baddies with the awesomeness that is Velm, we have to spend his skill points first.

  • Inn - the yellow tent you're standing in right now
  • Potions, Weapons, and Armor - cube-shaped blue-and-white tent (whoo that's a lot of dashes)

Shopping List

  • Weapons/Armor:
    • Note to self: thing about bonuses over power still applies. Only buy stuff if the thing someone's holding has no bonuses. For example, if Talinia's tunic has no bonuses (i.e. it's store-bought), upgrade. Else, don't upgrade.
  • Inn: Won't need to stay at inns anymore. *woohoo*

After you've done a bit of shopping, you're ready to leave town. Don't worry, we'll be back. Before we get adventuring, let's test out Velm. At the beginning of each battle, Velm should always throw up Group Shields (that didn't sound quite right, but you know what I mean.) Talinia should use Multiple Targets, then everyone should go all-out. Mipsy will start learning Group Haste soon, so she should cast that at the beginning of battle. After that, everyone should just go all-out offensive, with Velm using Group Heal whenever anyone (that's right, anyone) is hurt. If no one's hurt he can use Mesmerization.

Enough lecturing, let's get playing. Switch to Normal mode (you can be on Hunting if you want, but the baddies here don't give out that much EXP.) Now, before we start wandering aimlessly, a word from our sponsors.

The (not so) NEW Left-Hand Method!

Feeling LOST? Don't be! With our patented new Left-Hand Method, you can get out of ANY cave, guaranteed!

Just put your left hand on a wall and start walking! That's right! As long as you keep your left hand on a wall, you'll get out of the cave pronto!

But wait! There's more! This method will also work in towers and dungeons! So what are you waiting for?!?!?

**Brought to you by the NQII Help Club!**

DISCLAIMER: Method does not work if you are eaten by Chias before you leave the cave. And yes, you can use your right hand if you want. And stop reading small print, it's bad for your eyes.

Do you remember it? Maybe not, but that doesn't really matter. Just know that it works for mountains too. Pretend mountains are the walls, and you can find your way around pretty easily.

Well, now you're in the Valley of Kings. If you haven't found it yet, glance at your map for the Northwest Lost Desert. Find your map of this valley and look for the Palace of Ancient Kings in the southwest. Walk on over to the palace, then enter it.

palace of
ancient kings

You know, there's nothing I hate more than really long place names. Except for fish, but that's another story.

Just an FYI: we want to gain a level in this place. Use the Fight-As-You-Go method all throughout the palace. Once you grow to level 35/36/37, feel free to keep on hunting. But first we need to get going.

If you look at your map for Level 1, you'll notice that there are no stairs. (There is no spoon, either.) Well, the stairs are actually hidden underneath a bed. Chances are your map will have circled the bed, but if not, look for the weird pools of water and the rooms surrounding them. See the northwestern room? The bed is the one in the southwest of that room.

So take those stairs into Level 2. You've been plopped in a little maze, but I'm pretty sure you'll be able to get out OK. Once you get out into the dirt passageway, head north until you see your next boss.

the revenant

Notes: The Revenant has two cronies who are possessed skeletons (180HP on Normal, 270HP on Evil, 360HP on Insane.) Once you beat the Revenant, his cronies will die too, so don't bother attacking them.
  • Strategy:
  • General: Normally this guy would be pretty tough, but you have Velm now, so he's pretty easy. He's good practice for later bosses, though.
  • Setup: At the start, Velm should put up Group Shields while everyone else throws slowing potions at the Revenant until one works. After that, you can just go all-out offensive. Just remember to reapply potions/shields when they wear off.
  • Offensive: This guy's pretty easy so don't use any damage potions on him. Rohane and Talinia should use their melee while Mipsy nails the Revenant with Direct Damage.
  • Defensive: The Revenant can hit decently hard, but that's what Velm's for. Velm should use Group Heal almost every turn. (If no one's hurt by the time Velm's turn comes, Velm can mesmerize one of the cronies.)

Post-battle:

Nice work. Walk northeast of where the Revenant stood and talk to Lifira, who's evidently Waset's missing librarian. She'll teleport away. Now go northwest of where the Revenant was and you'll see another teleporter. That'll whisk you outside this palace.

treasure hunt

No RPG is complete without a bit of cheesy treasure-hunting.

We'll get to the treasure hunt soon enough. First, though, we need to get back to Waset. Switch to Normal mode and make your way back. Once you do, replenish your potions in the blue-and-white-cube tent. Then walk over to the red tent and talk to Lifira in the east part of the house. She'll tell you some story line and some secret word or other.

Go ahead and open up your maps for the Northwest Lost Desert or something like that. You're in Waset, and you need to get to the hermit toward the northwest of the map. He's standing next to a pitifully small patch of water and some trees. We're going to use the Fight-As-You-Go method to grow to level 36/37/38 as soon as we can. Make your way northwest (try to hug the mountains) to the hermit.

Once you get to the hermit, talk to him, of course. He'll give you pretty good (if annoying) instructions to get to where you need to. Follow those instructions and you'll find the 2nd piece of the Medallion of Wind (you know, that junk piece of metal you've been lugging around all this chapter.) If you haven't gained that level already, you'll want to train here until you do.

Now we need to get to our next boss, who's a good distance away. You know what that means. Switch to Hunting mode and keep using the Fight-As-You-Go method until you grow to level 37/38/39. You'll want to start off by walking southeast.

After a bit you'll reach the East Lost Desert (these names are really creative.) Ignore the weird rock formations (and, for that matter, the slightly creepy Skeith guy) and make your way to the Lost City of Akhten-Ka in the southwest. Keep beating up those baddies until you've leveled up. Once you get to Akhten-Ka, enter it.

akhten-ka

Ironically, the abandoned cities are all bigger than the real ones.

When you open up the map of this place, you'll be like a farm boy in New York. Which is to say, you get mugged almost immediately you feel really small. Unfortunately all the guys here want to mug you, so yeah. We have to grow to level 38/39/40 in this place. Walk on over to the northwestern corner of the city while using the Fight-As-You-Go method.

Once you reach the corner, gain that level if you haven't yet. Then look for the ghost guy Coltzan. Proceed to bust that ghost. (What, stop giving me weird looks like that. It's scary.)

coltzan's ghost

Wot? Where am I, and why am I fighting a fat Blumaroo and his bunch of Star Trek nerds?

  • Strategy:
  • General: Not too exciting, but are you gonna let him call you a Star Trek nerd? Didn't think so. Yeah, he's just a practice battle.
  • Setup: You know what to do. Have Rohane and Talinia toss slow potions and make sure he's always slowed. Velm should keep shields up, and if you really want to Mipsy can use Group Haste (it's probably too weak to help right now, though.)
  • Offensive: He can heal, yeah, but you can probably deal around 150HP in one turn. Remember that he called you a Star Trek nerd, and proceed to beat him to a spectral pulp.
  • Defensive: Coltzan's cool and all but he can't hurt you very much. Velm won't be using Group Healing that much, so he can toss damage potions if he wants. Don't bother with Mesmerization, since it doesn't work very often on bosses.

Post-battle:

Unfortunately, Coltzan hangs around even after you beat him up. He'll tell you to look at the barrel behind him, so when you do you'll get the gemstone of the medallion. Somehow I thought this would be a more rewarding experience. Anyway, there's no teleporter here so you'll have to walk out yourself. Stay on Hunting mode, though, since the baddies give nice EXP..

across the desert

Maybe it's just me, but the sections keep getting shorter. I don't know.

Before the end of this section, we need to grow to level 39/40/41, so use that Fight-As-You-Go method until you level up. Well, you've left the city, so open up your map of the East Lost Desert. See that weird rock formation (you know, the weird smiley face on its side)? Walk over to the "nose". 3 spaces west of the nose is the last piece of the Medallion. Walk over to it and grab it.

Now for that walk across half the desert. Open up your map to Lost Desert: Overall. You're still at that weird rock formation, but you have to get to Zakharukh's Pyramid in the far southwest. First, though, walk over to Waset. There, go into the blue tent. Restock your potions (remember, we need all the slowing/damage potions we can get) and sell your old weapons/armor here.

From there, go southwest to the Pyramid and enter it.

zakharukh's pyramid

Since I can't think of anything witty to say, please enjoy this picture of a dancing Grarrl.

All right, this is the last dungeon of the act. Go ahead and scroll down a bit to get the Grarrl off your screen, then switch to Hunting mode. Yup, we'll be using the Fight-As-You-Go method to grow to level 40/41/42 on this level. That's your last Lost Desert level. *woohoo*

Anyhow, look at the map for Level 1. Not too pretty. I'm just going to say good luck getting to Level 2. (I'm so nice, aren't I? And so modest too.) Level 2 isn't too bad by comparison, so make your way through it (nothing much to say about it, really.) Once you're on Level 3, heal up and get ready to face the final boss of this act.

anubits

  • Strategy:
  • General: This guy's pretty tough, with a powerful melee, high HP, and lots of goodies. You have Velm, though, and your other guys are hitting pretty hard by this point. This is a tough but doable battle.
  • Setup: Toss some Potions of Loafing Laziness (yeah, save those potions from Waset) to start and keep him slowed. Velm should put up shields, while Mipsy should use Group Haste (it'll be decent by this point.)

  • Defensive: Velm's probably got your back here, but if Anubits hits one character really hard make sure they heal with potions.

Post-battle:

Once you've beaten Anubits and reached that Altar of Density Destiny thingy (I still have no idea what it is), you'll be done the Lost Desert. You're now 2/3rds done the game, level-wise. I say we call it a day and go drink some, uh... soda. Yeah, soda.
