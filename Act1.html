Oh, hi again. So you've read all the guides and are raring to start killing those Chias. (Right? Right? If not, go to the guide page right now, mister. Or miss.)

As I was saying, you probably feel invincible with your knowledge of Obliterating stuff, throwing potions at stuff, and being all-around 1337. (OK, maybe not that last part.) But before you start, you've got to read the last little bit. I promise, it really is the last thing before the walkthrough. So scroll down a bit to see the Walkthrough Tips.

walkthrough tips

You can't just start playing NQII willy-nilly like that. You'll be eaten alive before you can say Don't eat me bro! So read these tips. And remember to follow them.

  • Ditch IE
  • Still using Internet Explorer? Hate to break it to ya, but it's slow and insecure, and it doesn't display pages right (especially this guide.) Download a better browser such as Firefox or Chrome. Just do a web search on them.
  • Use Maps
  • This is important. Without maps you'll get lost and become a tasty snack for Lupes/Chias/whatever. Do an internet search for NQII Maps and go to the first site you see. Get to know your way around and bookmark the site, cause we'll be coming back to that site a lot.
  • Block those ads
  • Banners get annoying, fast, as you play NQII. They take forever to load. So let's block 'em. Do an internet search on Block Ads for [your browser]. Download the AdBlock thingy.
  • Stop at good spots
  • I know you have a life so you're gonna have to stop playing NQII sometimes. Choose good stopping spots, such as towns or (best of all) the end of a walkthrough section. Don't stop playing in the middle of a cave/wilderness, if possible. If you like you can write down where you stopped so you aren't disoriented when you start playing again.
  • Hit F11
  • Look at the top of your keyboard. See those F keys? Hit F11 (on newer laptops, you might need to his Fn + F11.) Notice that the toolbars at the top of your screen go away, letting you see more of the actual page. (Just hover your mouse at the top of the screen to get the URL bar/tabs.) Hit F11 to go back to normal browsing mode. Always play NQII on F11 mode.
  • Buy wisely
  • Gold doesn't grow on trees, after all. Only buy weapons/armor/potions if you need it. Never buy weapons for Mipsy/Velm (they never use melee anyway.) If someone's carrying a weapon with bonuses, don't buy a weapon unless the weapon for sale has a big power/defense boost from the one they're carrying (I'm talking 4+ points.) If you never use damage/haste potions, don't buy them!

See, that wasn't so hard, now was it? Now we can (finally) start playing. *applause*

Trestin Village

Home sweet home.

All right, we're ready to start! First off, click me to start a game. Keep hitting "Continue" until you get to Trestin Village. But before we start actually playing, we're going to die. Yes, you heard me right. Click on "Hunting" next to "Travel" so that Hunting gets bolded. Congrats! You've just switched to Hunting mode. Now "switch" back to Normal mode. Now back to Hunting. You're learning already.

Now to, uh, get killed. Walk west from where you start so you leave town. Then walk back and forth a bit until you run into a Plains Lupe. Start the fight and then hit Wait 5s each time your turn comes. Eventually you'll die and be spirited back to Trestin. Congrats! By dying to the weakest baddie in the game, you got an avatar. And they say NQII is hard. Pfft.

Now we're going to take revenge. *cackles* OK, so stay on Hunting mode (stay is a hard vocab word: it means to not change your travel mode! Gasp!) Anyway. Walk outside town but don't go anywhere yet. Walk back and forth (go west one step, then east one step... or north, then south... the possibilities are endless.) When you run into a Plains Lupe, kill it. If your HP bar turns yellow (ooh look this isn't yellow... scary), switch to Normal mode and walk back into town. Talk to mom and she'll heal you to full health for free. She is your mom after all. Then switch back to Hunting and start, well, hunting again.

Keep beating up those Lupes until you grow to level 2 if you're playing Normal mode, 3 if Evil, and 4 if Insane. (See what I wrote? That was 61 characters. I'm lazy so I'll write in the format 2/3/4 from now on. If you don't understand the syntax, just hover your mouse over the 2/3/4.) If you get hurt, run back to mom.

Once you grow to level 2, you're going to get a skill point (you read about that, right?) Now to spend it. Look to your right and you'll see a table called "skills." (Wonder what that could be...) Look to the row that says level 2 and look right next to the 2. See the words Damage Increase next to it? (If you want to see more info, just hover your mouse over the skill name.) Now go to your skills page (when you're in the overworld, look right next to your health bar. Bada bing.) Look at the Damage Increase row and go across till you see 1: Fighting Stance in big red letters. Click on the red letters and confirm. Congrats, you just spent a skill point!

OK, from now on I'm not going to waste a paragraph telling you how to spend skill points. If you're cool enough to read this guide in the first place, you're smart enough to have caught on by now. So remember, right now you're at level 2/3/4 and you've spent all the skill points you've earned. Now we can move on. *cheers*

Moving On

No no, you WERE a weakling. Not anymore.

So you've gotten your (now useless) avatar and are ready to start beating up tougher stuff. Good news, you can. So leave town on Normal mode and go north into some hills. We're going to do some training here. What's that? You'll find out.

Basically, all training is is walking around on Hunting and fighting. Not that glamorous, eh? Yeah. Well now that you're in the hills, practice some of this training stuff. If your HP goes down so that your HP bar turns yellow/red (I prefer the more technical you get hurt), switch to Normal mode and run back to mom. When you're healed start training again.

Once you level up to level 3/4/5, spend your skill point. Now go to your map site and open up the Western Plains map. See that cave in the northwest corner? Yeah, you wanna go there. Right now you're near Trestin Village. So switch to Normal mode (whenever I say something like that, I mean Normal travel mode, not Normal difficulty) and walk your way over to the forest near the cave. If you run into baddies just kill 'em.

Now that you're in the forest (right?) start training. I hope you remember what that means. If you're hurt, use those Healing Vials everyone seems to carry. You know how to do this in battles and in the overworld. All right? So grow to level 4/5/6 in this forest. Spend your skill point (I think you're getting annoyed by this... so I'll stop.) And, uh, look at that. We're at the end of the section already. And it only took us 4 paragraphs. You're turning pro already! *fist bump*

Dark Cave

All caves are dark, but this one's official.

I admit the first two sections took a while to read. I'll stop babying you after this section. I promise (I'm being serious for once.) Oh yeah, the game. But first a word from our sponsors.

The NEW Walk-Heal Method!

Feeling LOW on HP? Well just don't stand there, do something!

Just walk around to regain HP! That's right, just walk around! Now for every 7 steps you take after battle, you regain 1 HP! That's up to 7 HP after each battle, yours positively FREE! So what are you waiting for?!?!?

As an added bonus, you don't even have to win the battle! You can flee and still gain HP afterwards!

**Brought to you by the NQII Help Club!**

DISCLAIMER: Method does not work if you are attacked or eaten by Chias before 7 steps is up. No, that Kacheek won't stop walking. And stop reading small print, it's bad for your eyes.

Uh, sorry about that. I didn't recommend that color scheme or that Kacheek to them. Anyway, use the walk-heal method after each battle. The few HP adds up in the long run.

OK, so you've probably scrolled down a lot to get that annoying walking Kacheek off your screen. If you get lost in this cave (hopefully you won't), use the left-hand method you read about. Put your left hand on a wall and walk, keeping your hand against the wall the whole time. But we'll come back to this later.

Now open up your map site to the Dark Cave/Gold Mine map. You're in the northwest, you wanna get into the building in the southwest. So switch to Hunting mode and make your way through the cave (I think you know how to), beating up the baddies as they come. Don't walk back and forth or anything, just beat them up if they get the nerve to attack you. Use a Healing Vial if you're hurt.

So keep on walking and chewing gum (not required) until you land on some train tracks. Before you move on, train to level 5/6/7. Once that's done, spend the skill point take the southwest fork in the road.

Look at your map site and look at the 4-way intersection in the west of the building. Get there and train to level 6/7/8 once you're there. Use only Healing Vials here, don't use those funky new Healing Flasks. The minute you level up, switch to Normal mode, do not pass go, do not collect 200 NP. Instead go south until you see a big blue lard tub. Say hi to your first boss and then get ready to kill him. Haha.

the miner foreman

Yeah, he's about as fat as four men.
Recommended Level: 6 on Normal
7 on Evil
8 on Insane
HP: 50 on Normal
62 on Evil
75 on Insane
Special Abilities: None. Lucky you.
Prizes: 2 Healing Flasks
1 weapon/armor
100 NP
  • Strategy:
  • General: Since this is your first boss battle, this is also your first encounter with this sort of section. It's pretty simple, but know that Special Abilities are things like Direct Damage, Healing, and those sorta goodies. As for the Foreman, he isn't so bad. Just follow the Offensive and Defensive tips to win.
  • Offensive: Here's how to, well, go on the offensive. For the Foreman, just use your melee attack whenever you aren't healing. Which reminds me of the next part...
  • Defensive: This is the last part of the strategy. If the Foreman gets your HP down to yellow/red, use Healing Flasks on your turn. After that turn you can go on attacking.

Post-Battle:

Once he's got the skull on him, give yourself a pat on the back. You've just beaten your first boss. He'll drop either a sword or armor, so go to your Inventory and equip that. After that, step into the teleporter behind him to get warped outside the cave.

Open up your map site to the Western Plains map for a second. See that one-square-wide land-bridge thingy to the west? Switch to Normal mode and make your way over to that.

Once you're there, open the Northern Marches map. See the town of White River in the south? Walk there on Normal mode, beating up any Gelerts who get in your way. Step onto the city...

White River

The river's blue, not white. Why must you lie to us, NQII?!? *sobs*

...and you'll get warped into town. You can sightsee if you like but that's not why we're here. We're going to do some shopping. But first we're gonna rest cause we're cool like that.

Walk south a few steps. See the conspicuously marked inn to your east? Yeah. Enter that and talk to the Tonu behind the counter (yes, you can talk to him across the counter.) Ask him to stay there. The good part is that if you die before you beat the next boss, you'll be revived here instead of in Trestin. Saves you a good bit of walking.

So exit this inn and go across the street to the weapons/armor/potions shop. Talk to the dude behind the counter. You're going to buy either a sword or armor... but it depends on what the Miner Foreman gave you. If he gave you a sword, buy the armor. If he gave you armor, buy the sword.

Now go back to your inventory and equip what you just bought. Talk to Tebor again and buy 10 Blast Potions. Also buy as many Healing Vials as you can carry. Just keep clicking Buy 10 next to Healing Vials until you get a red error message. Once the shopping's done, we can leave town.

Now open up your map of the Northern Marches. See the forest northeast of White River? Well, switch to Hunting mode, leave town, and train in the forest until you're level 7/8/9. If you're hurt you can rest at the inn. Save your Healing Vials.

Once you've leveled up, switch to Normal mode and look at your map again. See the entrance to the Underground Cave in the northeast? Make your way over there and step on the entrance to the cave.

underground cave

They coulda built a bridge, but NO, they make you go through this cave.

Smelly. Well, at any rate, we're gonna train around the entrance of the cave until we're level 8/9/10. Use Healing Vials if you're hurt, save those Healing Flasks.

So once you've leveled up, you're ready to start moving through the cave. That's coming up, right after these messages.

The NEW Left-Hand Method!

Feeling LOST? Don't be! With our patented new Left-Hand Method, you can get out of ANY cave, guaranteed!

Just put your left hand on a wall and start walking! That's right! As long as you keep your left hand on a wall, you'll get out of the cave pronto!

But wait! There's more! This method will also work in towers and dungeons! So what are you waiting for?!?!?

**Brought to you by the NQII Help Club!**

DISCLAIMER: Method does not work if you are eaten by Chias before you leave the cave. And yes, you can use your right hand if you want. And stop reading small print, it's bad for your eyes.

Uh... yeah. Again, not my idea. Just use the left-hand method if (when) you get lost. Anyhow, now open up your map to the Underground Cave. You're in the northwest corner, and you need to get to the southeast corner. Switch to Hunting mode and make your way through the cave, beating up any baddies you run into. Just follow the puddles.

OK, so once you get to the end of the cave, stop, do not pass Go, do not collect $200. We're actually going to gain 2 levels here. So train up to level 10/11/12. If you're hurt, use Healing Flasks or Healing Vials if you really want to. Just save those Healing Potions (yeah there's a kind of healing potion called a Healing Potion.)

Once you've leveled up you can leave the cave. Woohoo! Now you're on a bite-size island, and you'll see a tower thingy nearby. Walk onto it to enter.

Mysterious Tower

Nothing witty to say here. It's just... a tower...

Enter that but don't go too far. Yeah, we have to train to level 11/12/13 at the entrance of the tower. Keep using those Healing Flasks cause we want to save the Healing Potions.

Once you've leveled up, switch to Normal mode and open your map to Mysterious Tower, Level 1. Walk over to the stairs in the northeast corner of the room, running away from any skeleton dudes you run into.

Now you're on Level 2 of the tower. Change your map accordingly. Find the stairs in the southeast and go up to Level 3. Good news! There's a new baddie for you to beat up, and he'll give out more EXP than the skeleton dudes. Unfortunately he hits kinda hard so use those Healing Flasks. Use as many as you like, actually, cause we won't be needing them much anymore.

Train up to level 12/13/14 (don't worry, that's the last level you have to gain before you face Zombom. I'm serious.) Glance at your Level 3 map if you need to and walk onto the stairs. Now you're on Level 4 of the tower, and Zombom's just south of you. Switch to Normal mode, heal up if you need to, and walk on down.

zombom

Sorry, FireFox, but even you lose out to MagicPeanutEater.
Recommended Level: 12 on Normal
13 on Evil
14 on Insane
HP: 120 on Normal
150 on Evil
180 on Insane
Special Abilities: Decimate - 31HP Direct Damage
Zeal - 18% Haste
Aid - 40HP Heal
Prizes: 3 Healing Flasks
1 weapon/armor
100 gold
200 NP
  • Strategy:
  • General:This peanut-eating pachyderm is a tough cookie (never thought you'd hear that in a NQII Guide, eh?) The fight isn't that complicated... just toss Blast Potions when you're not using Healing Potions. attack when you're not healing. Still, chances are you'll die to him... if you do, read the Post-Battle section.
  • Offensive: Blast Potions will hit harder than your melee attack most of the time, so use those (just click on the words Blast Potion) when you're not healing. If you start running low on them just start attacking normally.
  • Defensive: Zombom's Decimate will nail you for 31HP most of the time. On top of that, he'll double you (he gets 2 moves after each one of yours) sometimes, so always keep your HP above 62 (31 * 2.) Use those Healing Potions whenever your HP is below 62.

Post-Battle:

If you died...

Oof, sorry to hear that. You'll respawn in White River. If you have fewer than 5 Blast Potions left, buy 5 more. Then switch to Normal mode and open up your map site to the Northern Marches. Enter the cave (and open up your map for that), and make your way through it. Then go through the Mysterious Tower. You can get your rematch with Zombom at the top.

If he died...

Nice job! Not many people can beat the fat elephant (yes, I have tons of nicknames for Zombom.) Equip the new sword/armor he gave you and step onto the teleporter. You'll be outside the tower. Open up your map for the Underground Cave and switch to Normal mode. Follow the puddles back to the exit in the northwest. You're now in the Northern Marches. Make your way back to White River.

Congrats! You've just finished Act 1, Part 1. For more baddie-bashing fun (now with the help of Mipsy, the wizard with attitude), visit the Act 1.5 Guide on Fordulat's page. So it looks like you won't be coming back to this page anymore... looks like goodbye for now. *sniffle*
